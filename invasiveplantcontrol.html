Invasive Plant Control

Piedmont Environmental employs different techniques to control different invasives, depending upon time of the year and type of plant. Continued management is needed following an aggressive assault on an infested area.

What Are Invasive Plants?

Invasive Exotic Pest Plants are plants that have been introduced into our region from other areas. They have aggressive growing characteristics that threaten the survival of our native species. In their native regions, climate, animals, and disease organisms kept their growth in check. Here, they have not experienced those factors and have choked out native plants that have carefully evolved with their surroundings.

Examples of Invasive Plants

Chinese Privet

A dense, fast-growing shrub that invades forests and displaces native plants, forming impenetrable thickets.

Elaeagnus

A vigorous spreader with silvery leaves that overtakes natural areas, outcompeting native vegetation and altering habitats.

English Ivy

Climbs and overtakes trees, preventing photosynthesis and damaging bark.

Japanese Stillgrass

Spreads rapidly and forms dense mats, crowding out native plants and disrupting forest regeneration.
